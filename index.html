Projects

As an avid music fan, I was disappointed that there was no way to share music that I liked with my friends or to find out what kind of music my friends were listening to. This inspired me to create AudioShare, an iOS application where users can create a profile and share music they've been listening to recently. Their followers can like, comment, and share songs that you post and you can do the same to all the friends you follow. AudioShare was written entirely in Swift and the user datas are stored in a Parse server using an EC2 instance on AWS. Click on the logo to check out my project on Github.

GE.DU is an Android application created to help young students to master their geometry skills. Users can create accounts to help keep track of their progress. The app presents lessons to help students learn new material. After students have read the lesson, they are presented with a quiz to test their mastery of the material.

Ultimate Frisbee

I first picked up tossing around a frisbee when a few of my friends and I would toss around during lunch at high school. We finally decided to create an Ultimate Frisbee club our senior year and got about 60 to people to join. This passion for the sport carried right over into college and I have spent the last four years as a member of the UCSD Ultimate Frisbee Club Team. My team freshman year qualified for the 2014 National Championship Tournament in Cincinnati as we were ranked 13th in the nation. I've made some great friends along the way and would love to spread my love of ultimate frisbee with the world.

Founding Father of the Phi Delta Theta Fraternity

In the spring of 2015, my buddy mentioned a new fraternity being founded on our campus at UCSD. Initially, I was not too keen on the idea of a fraternity, especially since I had a brotherhood in the ultimate frisbee club team. But, I came to terms that this was too good of an opportunity to pass up. I could help make a real difference to how fraternities are viewed on campuses because we, as the founders, get to decide which direction to take this fraternity. Thus, I am proud to say that I helped found the California Kappa chapter of the Phi Delta Theta fraternity in May 2015 at UCSD. We are still going strong and I hold very strong ties to my brothers till this day.

Eagle Scout

I first became a cub scout at the age of 7 and became enchanted with everything about scouting: late night campfires and stories, summer camps, hiking to cool places, and helping the community. What wasn't there to love. Since then, I became a boy scout and moved up the ranks. Finally, by my senior year of high school, I was able to achieve the highest rank in scouting: Eagle Scout. For my project, I had built bleachers in the form of benches for the tennis team at my high school. Alternatively, the benches served as an area for students to wait while they waited to be picked up from school and taken home. I've gotten so much out of scouting and it feels good to be able to give back to the community. I still go hiking and camping regularly. Going forward, I strive to uphold the principles I've learned from scouting.
